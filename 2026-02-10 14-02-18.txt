Membrane Circles Model (Amphiphile Membranes) 
Modeling approach: 
Open system: Use a variable number of circles that can appear and disappear; a fixed circle count leads to unrealistic packing and porosity behavior. 
Phase targets: Demonstrate three states—no membrane, fluid, and solid—and evaluate whether transitions align with Diemer’s predictions. 
Membrane thickness and composition: 
Amphiphiles: Model membranes composed of monocarboxylic and dicarboxylic acids; a circle can represent a dicarboxylic acid or two linked monocarboxylic acids. 
Empirical inputs: Extract chain-length distributions for both classes from meteorite literature; sample chains randomly with the constraint that selections rarely exceed the membrane thickness. 
Interactions and metrics: 
van der Waals interactions: Define interaction strength for neighboring chains of differing lengths (e.g., whether the shorter length sets the interaction scale). 
Porosity metric: Porosity is the total area not covered by circles.

PCC (Pressure–Chaos–Control) Non-Transitive Dynamics (Exploratory)

Triad of strategies—pressure (aggression), control (optimization/prediction), and chaos (unpredictability)—forming a non-transitive rock–paper–scissors cycle.
